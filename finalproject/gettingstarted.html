Getting Started

  1. Learn about different kinds of vegetarians and decide what would work best for you. Decide what foods you do and don't want to avoid.
  2. Talk to your doctor and make sure that your diet won't have a negative impact onyour health.
  3. Start slowly, consider starting out by trying your new diet for a short period of time. Or you can choose to be vegetarian for certain days of the week, and slowly add more and more days once you are used to your new vegetarian diet.
  4. Try new foods, many cultures have delicious meatless recipes, and eating new foods can help to make being a vegetarian more fun
  5. Check your labels, when you have a highly restrictive diet it's eady to accidently eat to little protein and other nutrients. It is very important to make sure you are eating enough of the food you need!
  6. Try meal planning. Planning your meals in advance can help you to save money, save time, and ensure that you are eating food with enough nutrients (like protein, B12, and Omega 3).
  7. Try meat substitutes.
  8. Adjust your diet if it's negatively affecting your health.
  9. Find restaurants in your area with vegetarian or vegan options.
  10. Find vegetarian friends in-person or online, there are many supportive groups for vegans and vegetarians.
  11. Follow vegetarian blogs to find new recipes.
  12. Remember to always be kind to yourself!
